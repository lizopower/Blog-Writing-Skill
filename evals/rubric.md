# Blind Qualitative Rubric (Native-Voice)

Rate each draft 1–5 on each axis. Do this blind: no run labels, shuffled order.
The rater sees ONLY this rubric and the drafts — no skill files, no lint output.

## Axes

**1. Native cadence (1–5)**
Read one section aloud. 5 = rhythm of an American trade-press article: sentence
lengths vary naturally, short sentences land at logical peaks. 1 = metronome
prose, every sentence the same shape.

**2. Vocabulary naturalness (1–5)**
5 = plain verbs, concrete nouns, zero corporate-AI vocabulary, idioms (if any)
sound unforced. 1 = leverage/robust/seamless territory, or Latinate defaults
everywhere (utilize, commence, prior to).

**3. Translationese absence (1–5)**
5 = nothing reads as transfer from another language; articles (a/the) and mass
nouns are right. 1 = "with the development of", "more and more", "plays an
important role" patterns present.

**4. Specificity (1–5)**
5 = claims carry numbers, conditions, and sources; abstractions cashed out in
objects. 1 = "high quality", "excellent performance", unsourced statistics.

**5. Would-a-native-write-this (1–5, the gut check)**
Imagine this published on Protolabs' or DigiKey's blog under a named American
author. 5 = passes without a second look. 3 = competent but slightly "off".
1 = obviously machine or non-native.

## Recording

| Draft ID | Cadence | Vocab | No-translationese | Specificity | Gut check | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Median per axis per run; compare runs pairwise. Note WHICH sentences triggered
low scores — those become new lint candidates.
