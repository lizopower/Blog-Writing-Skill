# Understanding Injection Molding Tolerances

> **Style notes (why this is an exemplar)**
> - Opening: 3 sentences to the point, defines tolerance in-line, states the stake ("your parts won't fit or perform correctly") — no throat-clearing.
> - Rhythm: short punch sentences at irregular intervals ("Thick parts tend to sink." / "Resin selection is crucial." / "It's easy to do.")
> - Register: direct address + contractions ("Let's get into…", "that'll help", "you'll want to").
> - Numbers: imperial first, metric in parentheses — American B2B convention.
> - Sections end by advancing ("…and that might be a big problem.") not summarizing.

## Representative excerpts

Opening:

> While injection molding is a relatively precise process, there may be very
> slight variations between your design and your finished part. Tolerance is
> the range of deviation in specifications that still allows your part to
> function as needed. This is especially important when your project involves
> multiple components that need to fit together during assembly. If critical
> tolerances aren't met, your parts won't fit or perform correctly.

Punch-sentence rhythm (wall thickness):

> Thick parts tend to sink. Thick areas take longer to cool than the thinner
> areas. This can lead to divots and imperfections on the part exterior as the
> molten core shrinks inward and pulls the exterior walls with it.

Worked example with consequence (shrink):

> For example, say you've designed a mold for a part that will be manufactured
> using ABS plastic. ABS has a shrink rate of 0.003 in./in. (0.076mm/mm). […]
> Polypropylene shrinks at a faster rate than ABS, so the part will end up
> being roughly 0.015" smaller in./in. (0.381 mm/mm), and that might be a big
> problem.

Direct-address process guidance (CTQ):

> That's why we recommend that you call out up to five Critical-to-Quality
> (CTQ) features in your CAD model, including tolerances. It's easy to do.
> Before you submit your CAD file, use a red circle to indicate your part
> design's most critical features.
