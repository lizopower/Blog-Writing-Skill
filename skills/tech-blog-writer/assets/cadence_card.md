# Cadence Card — read this in full before drafting any English article

Verbatim sentences from verified native writers (sources in
`content/reference/american-voice/` and `industrial-b2b/`). This is your tuning
fork: read it aloud, then write. NEVER reuse these sentences — style only.

## Punch after setup (short sentence lands AFTER long ones)

> They're doing everything right. The form is perfect. It looks exactly the way
> it looked before. But it doesn't work. No airplanes land. — *Feynman*

> Today I want to tell you three stories from my life. That's it. No big deal.
> Just three stories. — *Jobs*

## Concrete object instead of abstraction

> I returned Coke bottles for the five-cent deposits to buy food with. — *Jobs*

> …a giant rocket more than 300 feet tall, the length of this football field…
> — *JFK*

## Claim with its condition attached (precision, not hedging)

> The voltage range of a single LFP cell is 2.5 V to 3.65 V, but from 90% to
> 10% state-of-charge, the voltage is between 3.1 V and 3.3 V. — *DiGiacomandrea*

> Typically, injection molds are machined to tolerances of ±0.127 mm. — *Melito*

## Cause → effect, no adjective inflation

> Thick parts tend to sink. Thick areas take longer to cool than the thinner
> areas. — *Protolabs*

> Out-of-tolerance molds result in out-of-tolerance parts. — *Melito*

## Aphorism AFTER evidence, never instead of it

> It's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair
> company at a wonderful price. Charlie understood this early; I was a slow
> learner. — *Buffett*

## Honest limitation, stated plainly inside confidence

> …made of new metal alloys, some of which have not yet been invented… — *JFK*

> We've never succeeded in making a good deal with a bad person. — *Buffett*

## Self-check before each section

Does your last paragraph vary sentence length like these? Is the shortest
sentence doing the most work? Is every abstraction cashed out in a number or
an object? If not, rewrite before moving on.
