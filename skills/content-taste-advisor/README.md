# Content Taste Advisor

A Claude skill that helps content creators develop and apply editorial judgment - the ability to distinguish what's worth making in the AI era.

## Overview

In a world where AI makes content creation effortless, the scarce skill is no longer **how to create** but **what's worth creating**. This skill helps you develop "taste" - sophisticated editorial judgment that can't be copied by competitors or AI.

## What This Skill Does

- **Evaluates content quality** beyond technical execution
- **Helps you choose topics** that serve your audience vs. chase trends
- **Finds distinctive angles** on common subjects
- **Develops your unique voice** and perspective
- **Builds your taste framework** through structured exercises
- **Challenges you** to make stronger editorial choices

## When to Use This Skill

Use `content-taste-advisor` when you need:

- Feedback on whether your content is distinctive or derivative
- Help deciding between multiple topic ideas
- A fresh angle on an overdone topic
- Guidance on developing your editorial voice
- A plan to systematically improve your judgment
- Understanding why your content isn't resonating

## Key Concepts

### The 7 Dimensions of Taste

1. **Topic Selection** - What you choose to cover (and refuse to cover)
2. **Framing & Angle** - How you approach common topics differently
3. **Structure** - Whether you design for your idea or default to templates
4. **References** - What sources you cite and how distinctive they are
5. **Voice & Tone** - Whether readers can identify your work without a byline
6. **Editing** - What you're willing to cut
7. **Timing** - When to speak and when to wait

### The 4 Quality Levels

- **Level 1**: Technical execution only (baseline)
- **Level 2**: Good audience fit (competent)
- **Level 3**: Distinctive perspective (taste evident) ⭐
- **Level 4**: Cultural resonance (exceptional taste) 🏆

### Core Principles

1. **Taste is not elitism** - It's defensible judgment, not obscurity
2. **Taste is learnable** - Through consumption, analysis, and practice
3. **Taste takes time** - Expect 100+ pieces before it becomes reliable
4. **Taste alienates** - Distinctive choices naturally repel some people
5. **Taste is your moat** - AI can copy execution, not judgment

## Taste Development Framework

### 1. Consume Prolifically
- Read/watch 100+ pieces in your domain per year
- Study the classics and history of your field
- Cross-pollinate from other domains
- Track what you consume and why it worked

### 2. Examine Why Things Work
Ask yourself:
- Why does this work? (Be specific)
- What do I like about it?
- How would I improve it?
- What would the "me" version look like?

### 3. Put in Your Reps
- Write 100 articles / Make 100 videos / Post 100 times
- Your first 50 pieces will mostly be bad - that's the point
- You're building pattern recognition through volume

### 4. Create Feedback Loops
- Publish publicly, don't work in stealth
- Talk to your audience about what resonates
- Track the right kind of engagement (replies, not just likes)
- Seek critical feedback from people who challenge you

### 5. Accept That Taste Alienates
- If everyone thinks your work is "pretty good," you're not making strong enough choices
- Polarization is a feature, not a bug
- Serve your audience deeply, not everyone superficially

## Practice Exercises

### The 3x3 Analysis (Weekly)
Find 3 pieces on the same topic (exceptional, mediocre, poor). Write 3 paragraphs for each explaining what makes it that quality level.

### The Reframe Challenge (Bi-weekly)
Pick a trending topic. List 5 common angles. Generate 5 uncommon angles by inverting assumptions or combining domains.

### The Voice Audit (Quarterly)
Mix your last 10 pieces with competitors' content. Can you identify yours without bylines? If no, identify what makes your voice generic.

### The Deletion Practice (Monthly)
Take a finished piece. Cut 20%, then another 10%. Is it stronger or weaker? Learn what's essential.

### The Reference Expansion (Monthly)
List your last 10 citations. Categorize as mainstream vs. distinctive. Find 2 alternative sources for each mainstream one.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Confusing taste with elitism** - Liking obscure things ≠ good taste
2. **Following best practices blindly** - Templates are starting points, not endpoints
3. **Chasing every trend** - Have confidence to skip what doesn't fit
4. **Optimizing for algorithms over audience** - Serve humans first
5. **Avoiding polarization** - Distinctive work naturally divides people
6. **Copying successful creators' voices** - Learn principles, not style
7. **Publishing without editing** - Delete ruthlessly

## Why This Matters in the AI Era

**The shift**:
- Old scarcity: Ability to create content
- New scarcity: Judgment about what's worth creating

**The opportunity**:
- Most brands will use AI to produce rivers of acceptable content nobody remembers
- Winning brands will slow down, develop point of view, and publish less but matter more

**Your advantage**:
- Competitors can steal your format and design
- AI can mimic your style and structure
- But they can't steal the judgment system that produced those choices
- Your taste is built on pattern-matching they haven't done
